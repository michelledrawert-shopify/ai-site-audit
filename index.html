Shopify Partner Enablement · Training walkthrough

Run an AI-generated site audit

Turn a prospect’s name and domain into an evidence-based commerce audit, a few sharp “why Shopify” hooks, and a ready-to-send outreach sequence that earns the first conversation — using tools your agency already has.

Works with Claude Code, Cursor, or ChatGPTOff-the-shelf web toolsNo Shopify-internal access needed
Prepared by Michelle Drawert, ShopifyA field-tested method, packaged to teach

The big idea

The method is the asset, not the tools

An AI agent doesn’t make an audit credible — the discipline does. Get these three things right and the specific tools become interchangeable.

The agent orchestrates; it doesn’t measure

Let the AI fetch pages, run tools, and write the report. The numbers come from real measurement tools, never from the model’s imagination.

The methodology is portable

The phased method — detect, diagnose, position, pressure-test, then arm the outreach — works on any platform with any toolchain. That’s what you’re really learning.

The discipline earns trust

Every claim labeled, every number sourced, every gap admitted. This is what separates a real audit from confident-sounding AI slop.

1Choose your agent

Pick the tool that fits how your team works

All three can research and draft. They differ in whether they can run the measurement tools for you, which decides how much you do by hand.

Terminal-based

Claude Code

Runs CLI tools (curl, the Lighthouse CLI, Screaming Frog’s CLI), edits files, and builds + commits the report. The closest match to a hands-off run.

Best for technical agencies

IDE-based

Cursor

Same agentic power inside a VS Code-style editor. Great if your developers already live in an IDE and want the report in a repo.

Best for dev-led teams

Browsing chat

ChatGPT / Claude / Gemini

Strong at research, synthesis, and writing, but can’t run local CLI tools — so you run Lighthouse, Wappalyzer, etc. by hand and paste results in.

Best for less technical teams

The one rule that makes any of them safe

The agent orchestrates and writes up; it never invents the numbers. If a tool didn’t produce a value, the agent labels it a gap. Bake that into the prompt (it’s already there) and you avoid the single biggest failure mode of AI audits.

2Follow the method

Twelve phases, one storyline

Detect what you’re looking at, diagnose it across every lens, distill a few sharp “why Shopify” hooks, pressure-test them honestly, then arm the agency with the questions and the outreach to open a conversation. The agent runs all of it in a single pass.

PHASE 0

Detect & route

Fingerprint platform, segment, and model; flag any revenue channel that transacts off-platform.

PHASE 1

Business & org

What they sell, positioning, size signals, key people, strategic signals.

PHASE 2

Stack & lock-in

Incumbent stack, integration debt, off-site systems of record (ERP/EDI); real switching cost.

PHASE 3

Performance

LCP/CLS/INP/TTFB vs benchmarks; be honest if speed is a strength.

PHASE 4

Checkout

Flow, wallets, payment methods, mobile friction, abandonment drivers.

PHASE 5

Site / UX

Nav, search, PDP/PLP, accessibility, trust signals, merchandising.

PHASE 6

CRO

Top 3-5 highest-impact conversion gaps, ranked.

PHASE 7

AI readiness

Structured data, feeds, discoverability by AI shopping agents.

PHASE 8

Positioning & hooks

Distill 3-5 sharp “why Shopify” hooks and the one lead wedge. The centerpiece.

PHASE 9

Honest read

The blunt case it might not land, then the fit call: pursue, wedge, or not now.

PHASE 10

First-conversation kit

Lead wedge, ranked hooks, smart questions, and objection responses.

PHASE 11

Outreach sequence

A short, multi-threaded email cadence grounded in the findings.

The two moves people skip

Phase 8 (Positioning & hooks) is the whole point — a few sharp, evidence-backed reasons to talk, not a feature dump — and Phase 9 (Honest read) pressure-tests them before you send. Skip them and you get a cheerful audit that doesn’t earn a reply.

4Keep it honest

The eight guardrails

These travel with the prompt. They’re the difference between an audit a prospect trusts and one they quietly discount.

Label every claim

FACT (observed or sourced) vs HYPOTHESIS (interpretation). The reader always knows which is which.

Confidence + basis

High / Medium / Low on every detection and estimate, each with a one-line reason.

Show your method

State the tool, device, and throttling behind every number. A metric with no method is noise.

Cite a source URL

Every external claim links out. Anything you can't source becomes an open item, not an assertion.

Never fabricate

No invented metrics, contacts, titles, or dates. A blocked tool is a labeled gap, full stop.

Validate vendor claims

Treat every lift/conversion claim, including the platform's own, as a hypothesis to test in a pilot.

Date your evidence

Note publication dates on case studies; flag anything older than ~18 months as potentially stale.

Capture before you render

Finish the full analysis before building the deliverable. Under pressure, compress reasoning, not sections.

5Ship the deliverable

Two deliverables, not one

The run produces TWO self-contained artifacts — keep them separate. A polished client-safe audit carries ~90% of the substance; a short internal kit holds the honest read and the outreach emails. Default to self-contained HTML you can host and screen-share; export to Google Slides or Docs if the buyer wants a deck.

Internal · never shared

Internal outreach kit

A one-pager: the fit call, the lead wedge, the ranked hooks, smart discovery questions, the top objections + responses, and the multi-threaded email sequence. The selling overlay, not a mirror of the audit. Marked “do not share with the prospect.”

Prospect-facing

Client-safe audit

The findings (Phases 0–7), the scorecard, and the “why Shopify” hooks — framed constructively, leading with the biggest finding. No honest-read, no objection handling, no scoring, no emails. This is the asset you walk a prospect through.

What goes in each

Internal kit: fit call (pursue / wedge / not now), lead wedge, 3–5 ranked hooks, smart discovery questions, top objections + responses, the honest read, and the multi-threaded email sequence.

Client-safe audit: hero, executive summary (leads with the biggest finding), scorecard (lens | finding | severity), Phases 0–7 findings, the “why Shopify” hooks + “what changes / what you gain” summary, smart questions, a soft next step — plus your agency’s branding.

What a run produces

Two self-contained HTML files, ready to host or screen-share:

Client-safe audit: a hero, a filterable scorecard, the findings framed constructively, and the “why Shopify” hooks — leading with the single biggest finding.

Internal kit: the fit call, the lead wedge, the ranked hooks, smart questions, objections + responses, and the outreach email sequence.

The reusable core

The audit prompt

Paste this into your agent, fill in the two inputs (plus any tool exports you already have), and let it run. This is the whole method in one block — copy it and make it yours.

ai-site-audit.prompt
You are a senior ecommerce strategist and sales-development partner helping a Shopify Partner
agency EARN THE FIRST CONVERSATION with a prospect. From ONLY a prospect's company name and
primary domain, autonomously research the company and its live site, then produce an
evidence-based audit whose JOB is to arm the agency with (a) a few sharp, defensible "why
Shopify" positioning hooks and ONE lead wedge, and (b) a ready-to-send outreach sequence that
gets a reply and books a first call. This is a top-of-funnel prospecting asset, NOT a migration
plan or a delivery project plan - it is far too early for a 30/60/90. Deliver TWO clearly
separated artifacts: a CLIENT-SAFE audit and a short INTERNAL outreach kit, both as
self-contained HTML.

Be honest, not promotional. Build every hook from real evidence: tie it to something you
actually observed, connect it to a SPECIFIC Shopify capability and the business outcome it
unlocks, and treat every lift claim (including Shopify's own) as a hypothesis to validate.
Lead with the single biggest structural finding even if it is not a textbook audit lens, pick
value props that fit THIS business model (never pitch checkout conversion to a business that
does not sell online), and let "not a fit right now / wedge only" be a valid internal call.

REQUIRED INPUTS (the only two I provide)
- Prospect: {{Company Name}}
- Primary domain: {{https://www.example.com}}
Derive everything else from the live domain and public sources: platform, segment, model,
region, stack, contacts. Where you cannot measure something, research and estimate it,
labeled clearly. Once the run starts, do not ask me for more inputs.

CAPABILITIES (default: USE EVERY RESEARCH TOOL YOU ACTUALLY HAVE)
Before you start, take stock of what you can really do in THIS environment - web search,
fetching a live URL, running code / curl, a headless browser, any SEO or tech-stack APIs - and
USE all of it. Do NOT sit on a capability you have: a tool you could have run but didn't is a
worse failure than a labeled gap. Hard rules: never claim or guess a tool you do not have, and
never fabricate a value a tool would have produced (genuinely blocked or unavailable = labeled
gap).
Optional overrides (leave blank for a normal, full-power run):
[ ] Knowledge-only mode - ignore all live tools (use ONLY to test graceful degradation)
[ ] Exclude these tools (I do not have them here): __________
[ ] Paid / desktop tools I will paste myself (the agent cannot self-serve these): BuiltWith,
    Ahrefs, SEMrush, full Screaming Frog, Lighthouse - put their exports under SUPPLIED INPUTS

SUPPLIED INPUTS (OPTIONAL - only if you already ran a tool yourself; skip anything you don't have)
How to use: replace the "..." after a line with your paste, and delete the lines you have nothing
for. Keep it to SUMMARIES / overview tabs / top-N rows, never full raw dumps (large dumps overflow
context). Start each paste with the TOOL NAME + EXPORT DATE in brackets. Anything you leave out, the
agent researches itself (using its own live tools) and labels it. NOTE: this block is for tools the
agent CAN'T run itself - paid/desktop ones like BuiltWith, Ahrefs, SEMrush, Screaming Frog, or a
Lighthouse export - so paste what YOU exported and it is treated as measured fact. The agent already
uses its own live tools (web search, URL fetch, code) automatically; you don't declare those.
- Tech stack (BuiltWith / Wappalyzer): ...   (example: [BuiltWith, 2026-06-22] Platform: WooCommerce 8.x;
  Payments: Authorize.Net + PayPal; ESP: Klaviyo; Analytics: GA4 + Meta Pixel; Hosting: WP Engine)
- Crawl (Screaming Frog overview + issues): ...
- Traffic & keywords (Ahrefs / SEMrush top pages, organic traffic, top keywords): ...
- Performance (PageSpeed / Lighthouse scores + device/throttle, or JSON): ...
- Anything else, labeled with tool + date: ...

OPERATING MODE
- Single autonomous run. After the inputs above, do NOT ask questions, pause, or stop early.
  Run start to finish.
- Treat "current" as of today.
- Graceful degradation: if a tool or source is genuinely blocked, rate-limited, or unavailable,
  label the gap and proceed. A blocked source is a labeled gap, never a fabricated finding.
- Capture the FULL analysis for every phase BEFORE you build the report, so no section is
  thinned under pressure. Compress reasoning, never sections.

TOOLS (three tiers; use only what the capabilities declaration allows, do not guess)
Tier 1 - Host-agent native (default, if shell is available):
  - Live HTML & assets: fetch the homepage and key pages (curl). Read the <head>, scripts,
    link tags, robots.txt, and sitemap.xml; parse JSON-LD / structured data; fingerprint the
    stack from markup signatures.
  - Performance / Core Web Vitals: run Lighthouse (npx lighthouse, headless Chrome) and/or call
    the PageSpeed Insights API and CrUX API by curl (both public, no key) for field data.
    ALWAYS state device + throttling.
  - Accessibility: axe-core via a headless browser, or the Lighthouse accessibility score.
  - SEO: a lightweight crawl (titles, canonicals, 301s, catalog size) via script if no crawler.
  - Competitive proof: web search (if available) for named, dated outcomes; cite a URL for every
    claim, prefer named outcomes, note the publication date, and flag anything older than ~18
    months.
Tier 2 - Shopify Dev plugin / AI Toolkit (if available): use ONLY to ground the Shopify side of
  the pitch - validate every Shopify capability, feature, or API claim against current Shopify
  docs and schemas so nothing about Shopify is hallucinated. Its store-admin / GraphQL / Liquid
  functions do not apply to a prospect you have no store access to.
Tier 3 - Partner-supplied tools:
  - Paste-in exports (above) take precedence over your own estimates for that finding; say so
    ("upgraded from inferred to measured via <tool>, <date>").
  - Agent-run paid tools (Ahrefs / SEMrush / BuiltWith APIs, Screaming Frog CLI): attempt ONLY
    if declared above; otherwise treat as a labeled gap or rely on paste-in.

LABELING + PROVENANCE (use everywhere)
- Tag every claim FACT (observed/sourced) or HYPOTHESIS (interpretation).
- Confidence High / Medium / Low on detections and estimates, with a one-line basis.
- Provenance on every data point: agent-observed / partner-supplied (tool + date) / inferred.
- State the measurement method for any number (tool, device, throttling).
- Never invent a metric, contact, title, date, or figure. If unmeasurable, label it
  "estimated"/"inferred" with the basis. Never present inferred or supplied data as your own
  measurement.
- Treat ANY vendor conversion/lift claim (including Shopify's own) as a claim to validate.

SEGMENT DEFINITIONS (size tier is almost always inferred -> label HYPOTHESIS + confidence)
Infer from public signals only: catalog size, traffic/order-volume proxies, employee count
(LinkedIn), number of storefronts/locales/brands, funding/press, stack complexity.
- SMB: small catalog, single market, founder/small team. Angle: speed, ease, lower TCO than
  fragmented tooling, unified commerce fundamentals.
- Mid-market (MM): growing catalog, multi-channel, some international/B2B, notable traffic or
  funding. Angle: consolidation, conversion lift, channel unification, ecosystem depth.
- Enterprise (ENT): large catalog, multi-region/multi-brand, complex custom stack, high
  traffic/revenue signals. Angle: migration de-risking, TCO, unified commerce, developer
  velocity, scalability at volume.

PHASES
 0  Detect, route & find the real story - fingerprint platform/edition, hosting, headless vs
    monolith, payments, and point-solutions; infer size tier (per SEGMENT DEFINITIONS) and model
    (DTC/B2B/marketplace/retail/intl); select which value themes to RUN and which to SKIP, and
    why. CRITICAL: look for any major revenue channel that is REFERENCED on the site but
    transacts entirely OFF-PLATFORM (wholesale/B2B via a form or email, retail/POS not unified,
    subscriptions on a third party, marketplace/EDI). Flag these as PRIMARY OPPORTUNITY
    candidates, not just gaps - "the biggest part of this business is invisible to the platform"
    is usually the strongest hook. Lead the whole audit with the single biggest structural
    finding, even if it is not a standard lens.
 1  Business & org profile - what they sell, model, positioning, size signals, public key
    people, strategic signals (press, hiring, expansion, replatform rumors). A relevant new hire
    (e.g. a "Director of Ecommerce") is a buying trigger worth flagging.
 2  Incumbent stack & lock-in - full stack + custom builds + integration debt; flag what
    creates real switching cost. Also probe for off-site systems of record that shape any move
    (ERP, accounting, inventory/WMS, 3PL, EDI, CRM) - record them as discovery items, not
    assumed facts.
 3  Performance & Core Web Vitals - LCP/CLS/INP/TTFB (mobile + desktop) with method stated;
    page weight, render-blocking, vs category benchmarks; tie to conversion/SEO. Be honest if
    performance is a strength, not a wedge.
 4  Checkout audit - steps, friction, guest checkout, wallets/express, payment methods, mobile,
    likely abandonment drivers. (Skip or de-emphasize if they do not sell online.)
 5  Site / UX audit - nav, search, PDP/PLP, content depth, accessibility flags, mobile, trust
    signals, merchandising sophistication.
 6  CRO audit - PDP clarity, social proof, urgency, cart/upsell, search relevance, promos,
    personalization; identify the top 3-5 highest-impact gaps.
 7  AI / agentic readiness - structured data & feed readiness, discoverability/transactability
    by AI shopping agents, personalization, data-foundation maturity.
 8  POSITIONING & HOOKS (the centerpiece) - ONLY the themes activated in Phase 0.
    PLATFORM CASE FIRST: Before naming any specific capability, write 2-3 sentences establishing
    WHY Shopify is the right platform for this merchant's model and stage - independent of any
    specific feature or plan tier. This platform case should hold whether the merchant's primary
    interest turns out to be DTC, wholesale, events, or international. It is the frame that makes
    the specific hooks land.
    HOOK NAMING: Name every hook after the merchant's outcome or pain, not the Shopify product
    that solves it. "Shopify B2B" is not a hook. "Your dealer network is scaling past what manual
    ordering can handle" is a hook. The Shopify capability is the supporting evidence, not the
    headline.
    DTC PATTERN: If the merchant is primarily DTC with no dominant B2B/events/international gap,
    the hooks should address: checkout quality and wallet coverage, platform reliability and
    speed, ecosystem breadth (apps, integrations, themes), and merchant control - not wholesale
    or POS capabilities.
    For each hook, connect the finding to a specific Shopify capability and the business outcome
    it unlocks. Shopify capabilities to draw from as supporting evidence (not as hook titles):
    checkout/wallet gaps -> Shop Pay + Shopify Payments; recurring revenue -> Shopify
    Subscriptions; in-person/events -> Shopify POS; wholesale -> Shopify B2B; international ->
    Shopify Markets; speed/merchandising limits -> themes + checkout extensibility/Functions or
    Hydrogen; stack sprawl -> consolidation/TCO. DISTILL into 3-5 sharp "why Shopify"
    positioning hooks and name the SINGLE lead wedge. A strong hook is specific to THIS business,
    leads with the prospect's pain (not a Shopify feature list), implies a cost of standing
    still, and is credible (anchored to a FACT + a dated proof URL from shopify.com). Open by
    acknowledging what they already do well, then pivot to "the next phase" - never "your site
    is broken." Include: a compact "what changes / what you gain" summary (a few rows, not an
    exhaustive parity table); a one-line consolidation hook where it applies ("retire N plugins
    + a hosting contract, narrow PCI scope"); and 1-2 risk-based hooks where relevant (e.g.
    subscription payment-token portability) framed as "worth pressure-testing together," not a
    full risk register. Flag every lift claim as a hypothesis to validate.
    PLAN TIERS: Do not recommend a specific Shopify plan by name in either artifact - plan fit
    is a discovery conversation, not a prospecting claim.
 9  [INTERNAL] Honest read - a few blunt bullets: the strongest case this might NOT land, why the
    incumbent is sticky, what evidence is missing. Then the fit call: PURSUE / WEDGE-ONLY / NOT
    NOW. Keep it short; it exists to keep the hooks honest.
10  [INTERNAL] First-conversation kit - the single lead wedge to open with; the 3-5 hooks ranked;
    5-7 smart discovery questions, each with a one-line "why you're asking"; the top 2-3 likely
    objections + crisp responses (reframe, don't argue; don't enter the prospect's frame). You
    MAY add an optional 3-bullet "what a first engagement could explore" - but NO dated plan, NO
    30/60/90, NO MEDDPICC or buying-center map.
11  [INTERNAL] Outreach sequence - a short, multi-threaded email cadence grounded in the
    findings, ready for the agency to tailor. Vary by persona (economic buyer/owner, the channel
    lead e.g. head of wholesale, the ecommerce/ops manager). 3-4 touches: Day 1 insight-led with
    a short question-style subject line ("quick question about checkout"); a Day 3-5 bump that
    adds a second insight and offers the audit walkthrough; a final "should I close the loop, or
    is there someone else on your team I should talk to?" break-up. Each email: short, leads with
    ONE observed pain (not features), a soft CTA (15-20 min / a walkthrough). Reference only
    FACT-labeled observations, NO fabricated metrics, and leave sender/voice as placeholders the
    agency owns. Note channel mix where useful (voicemail + email bump, gifting) and aim subject
    lines for a real open rate (~30%+).

OUTPUT HANDLING (these two artifacts are large - do not let them truncate)
- If you can write files (Claude Code, Cursor, a shell): write EACH artifact to its own .html file
  instead of printing it into chat. This is the recommended way to run.
- If you can only print to a chat: output Artifact A in FULL, then STOP and wait; produce Artifact
  B only when asked to continue. Never cut off a file mid-way - if you are low on room, finish the
  current artifact cleanly and say so.

DELIVERABLE (TWO self-contained, responsive HTML artifacts; leave a slot for your agency's branding)
A) CLIENT-SAFE audit - the main, polished, prospect-facing asset you walk them through and leave
   behind. Sections in order: (1) hero with prospect / domain / detected platform-segment-model;
   (2) executive summary that LEADS with the single biggest structural finding; (3) scorecard
   (lens | finding | severity); (4) the findings from Phases 0-7, framed constructively; (5) the
   "why Shopify" case - the platform-level positioning first, then the hooks plus the compact
   "what changes / what you gain" summary; (6) smart questions to explore together; (7) a soft
   next step (offer a walkthrough). NO honest-read, NO fit verdict, NO objection handling, NO
   outreach emails, NO scoring. Do NOT mention any Shopify plan tier by name (Basic, Shopify,
   Advanced, Plus, Enterprise) - plan fit is a discovery conversation, not a prospect-facing
   claim.
B) INTERNAL outreach kit - short (about one page of substance), clearly marked "Internal - do
   not share with the prospect." Contains: the fit call, the single lead wedge, the 3-5 ranked
   hooks, the smart discovery questions, the top 2-3 objections + responses, the compressed
   honest read, and the multi-threaded email sequence. This is NOT a mirror of the audit - it is
   the selling overlay.
- Output each as a single self-contained HTML file you can open in a browser or host yourself
  (Vercel, Netlify, Cloudflare Pages, or GitHub Pages). Do not assume the agent has deploy
  credentials. Export the same content to Google Slides/Docs if the client prefers a deck.

QA GATE (self-verify before declaring done)
1. Both artifacts present; the client-safe audit LEADS with the biggest structural finding; all
   sections have real content (no placeholders).
2. Every claim labeled FACT/HYPOTHESIS; every estimate has confidence + basis; every number has
   a stated method; every data point has provenance. No unlabeled numbers.
3. Every external claim and case study has a dated source URL; unfound points are open items.
4. Phase 8 hooks trace back to themes activated in Phase 0; exactly ONE lead wedge is named; the
   value props fit the business model (no checkout pitch to a non-transacting site); no Shopify
   plan tier named in the client-safe artifact; the platform case appears before the feature hooks.
5. Every Shopify capability claim grounded against Shopify docs where available; every vendor
   lift claim (including Shopify's own) flagged to validate by test.
6. The CLIENT-SAFE artifact contains ZERO internal content (no honest-read, fit verdict,
   objections, scoring, or outreach emails).
7. Any off-platform revenue channel is elevated as a primary opportunity candidate, not buried
   as a gap.
8. The agent attempted only declared capabilities; all gaps are labeled, none fabricated.
9. Outreach emails reference only FACT-labeled observations; sender/voice are placeholders; no
   fabricated metrics, contacts, or dates.
10. NO 30/60/90 plan, MEDDPICC, or buying-center map anywhere - out of scope at this stage.

Before you run it for real

Pitfalls & data privacy

Don’t feed it confidential data

Auditing a public website is low-risk. But never paste a prospect’s private or customer data into a consumer AI tier — use business/enterprise tiers with model-training turned off.

Hallucination is real

The labeling + source-URL rules are the antidote. If a claim has no source and no FACT/HYPOTHESIS tag, treat it as suspect and cut it.

Tools get rate-limited

PageSpeed Insights and others throttle. When a tool is blocked, the run should record a labeled gap and move on, not invent a number to fill the hole.

Check recency

AI training data and cached pages go stale. Verify case-study dates and live-site facts at run time; flag anything older than ~18 months.
